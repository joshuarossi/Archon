01 · Principles

Five rules, in order of priority.

When two principles conflict, the earlier one wins. This is how we resolve design disagreements without another meeting.

01

Calm over clever

No saturated colors, no gamification, no celebration motion. The user is stressed — the UI should feel like a breath.

02

The human decides

Every AI output is a suggestion. The Send button is always a human action — never automatic.

03

Privacy is visible

If data is private, the screen says so. Trust is earned by showing the seams, not hiding them.

04

One primary action

Exactly one sage-filled button per screen. Secondary actions are visually subordinated.

05

Presence over polish

Streaming cursors, live updates, quiet activity. Invest motion in real interaction.

02 · Color

Warm neutrals, sage accent, lavender for the AI.

Every token is declared as a CSS custom property. Dark mode swaps values; components never reference hex directly.

10 · Synthesis card

The moment of pause before the joint session.

Displayed on the "Ready for Joint" screen. Generated per-party, never containing raw quotes from the other party.

Private to you — Jordan has their own version.

Areas of likely agreement

Both of you value the project's success, and both recognize the current timing tension is solvable. There's more common ground here than either of you may feel in the moment.

Points that will need real discussion

There's a genuine difference in how each of you prioritizes scope versus deadline. Expect to spend time on the trade-off, not just the calendar.

Suggested approach

Start by acknowledging what's working before raising the constraint question. Be specific about your hard dates; keep scope open as the shared variable.

11 · Draft Coach panel

14 · Copy rules

Content is design.

NEVER USE
  • — "arbiter", "verdict", "judgment"
  • — "the parties" (use names instead)
  • — 🎉 or exclamation marks from the AI
  • — "Get started free", pricing language
  • — "Congratulations on resolving your conflict!"
USE INSTEAD
  • — "coach", "guidance", "synthesis", "resolution"
  • — "You and Jordan"
  • — Steady, curious tone. The AI offers, suggests, wonders.
  • — Plain description of what the product does.
  • — "Case closed."
